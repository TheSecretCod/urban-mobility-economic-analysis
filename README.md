# urban-mobility-economic-analysis
Python-based analysis of urban mobility and economic productivity using exploratory data analysis and data visualization.

## Project Overview

This project explores the relationship between urban mobility indicators and economic productivity across major Latin American cities during 2024.

The analysis combines traffic congestion metrics, travel times, GDP per capita, unemployment indicators, and population data to identify behavioral patterns, outliers, and potential opportunities for transportation infrastructure improvements.

## Objectives

- Analyze the relationship between traffic congestion and economic productivity.
- Compare urban mobility indicators across Latin American cities.
- Identify outliers and unusual mobility patterns.
- Explore potential opportunities for transportation infrastructure investment.
- Generate business-oriented insights using exploratory data analysis.

## Technologies Used

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Insights

- No direct relationship was identified between higher GDP per capita and increased traffic congestion.
- Several cities showed significantly higher congestion levels compared to the regional average.
- Bogotá and Lima emerged as important cases for additional infrastructure and mobility analysis.
- Montevideo showed a combination of high GDP per capita and relatively low congestion, suggesting more efficient urban mobility conditions.

## Business Recommendations

- Prioritize mobility infrastructure analysis in cities with high congestion and lower economic productivity.
- Continue monitoring urban mobility indicators to support long-term transportation planning.

- Validate potential outliers to identify operational inefficiencies or data quality issues.
